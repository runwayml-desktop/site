Cookie Policy

Last Updated: June 15, 2024

1. Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how Ruwnay ("we," "us," or "our") uses cookies and similar technologies to recognize you when you visit our website. It explains what these technologies are and why we use them, as well as your rights to control our use of them.

2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small data files placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide reporting information.

2.1 First-party vs Third-party Cookies

  • First-party cookies are placed by us directly
  • Third-party cookies are placed by our partners and service providers

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function and cannot be switched off. They include:

  • Authentication cookies
  • Security cookies
  • Session management cookies

3.2 Performance Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by:

  • Collecting analytics data
  • Measuring page load times
  • Tracking user behavior patterns

3.3 Functional Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality by:

  • Remembering your preferences
  • Personalizing content
  • Storing your settings

3.4 Marketing Cookies

These cookies track your browsing habits to deliver targeted advertising, including:

  • Cross-site tracking
  • Ad performance measurement
  • Personalized recommendations

4. Cookie Management

4.1 Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • Block all cookies
  • Delete existing cookies
  • Allow only specific cookies
  • Configure site-specific settings

4.2 Our Cookie Preferences Tool

We provide a cookie management tool that allows you to:

  • View all cookies in use
  • Choose which categories to accept
  • Update preferences at any time
  • Withdraw consent for non-essential cookies

5. Specific Cookie Purposes

5.1 Analytics and Performance

  • Google Analytics cookies for traffic analysis
  • Load balancing cookies for performance optimization
  • Error monitoring cookies for system stability

5.2 Functionality

  • Language preference cookies
  • User interface customization cookies
  • Course progress tracking cookies

5.3 Security

  • Authentication tokens
  • Fraud prevention cookies
  • CSRF protection tokens

6. Data Retention

Cookie retention periods vary based on type:

  • Session cookies: Deleted when browser closes
  • Persistent cookies: Up to 12 months
  • Authentication cookies: 30 days maximum

7. Third-Party Services

We use various third-party services that may set cookies:

  • Google Analytics
  • Payment processors
  • Social media plugins
  • Video hosting services

8. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy periodically. Changes will be effective when posted on this page.
